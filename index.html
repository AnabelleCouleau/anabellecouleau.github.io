About me

I am currently working as a Consultant at the Inter-American Development Bank (IADB) in the Water and Sanitation Division with a focus on water access and quality, and waste related projects under extreme weather shocks.

During my doctoral studies at the University of Illinois, I started to work in the field of data science and machine learning, focusing on analyzing high-frequency data. Following my academic journey, I worked as a researcher and professor in Colombia. During this time, I developed projects addressing disaster risks, particularly landslides, and enhancing climate resilience for agricultural sectors, both crucial issues in tropical regions.

Driven by my deepening interest in data science and MLOPs (Machine Learning Operations), I made the transition from academia to become an independent consultant, dedicating myself to innovative projects at the intersection of climate data science and artificial intelligence.
