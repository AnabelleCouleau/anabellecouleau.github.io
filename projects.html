Research & Projects

Academic research and consulting work at the intersection of water economics, climate shocks, and food security in Latin America and the Caribbean.

Publications & Working Papers

Reports

Safeguarding the Sip: Drinking Water Quality Regulation, Monitoring and Surveillance in Latin America and the Caribbean
Couleau A., Perez-Urdiales M., and Poli L.
Inter-American Development Bank · Published April 2026
A comprehensive regional assessment (260 pages) of drinking water quality regulation and compliance across LAC countries, covering reporting, sampling adequacy, and violation share behaviors.

Working Papers

Weather Shocks and Food Prices in a Very Diverse Country: Evidence from Colombia  Submitted
Almeida A., Bohorquez-Penuela C., Bonilla-Mejia L., and Couleau A. (2025)
Uses granular wholesale price data (SIPSA) to identify the causal impact of extreme rainfall events on food prices across Colombia's heterogeneous geography.
When Does Rural Credit Improve Farm Socio-Ecological Resilience? Heterogeneous Treatment Effects among Colombian Smallholders
Zapata-Valencia R.S., Couleau A., and Muñoz-Mora J.C. (2025)
Estimates heterogeneous treatment effects of rural credit access on the socio-ecological resilience of smallholder farms in Colombia.

Consulting Projects

Applied research and policy analysis for the Water and Sanitation Division of the Inter-American Development Bank.

Water Quality Regulation in LAC

Regional assessment of drinking water quality regulation, monitoring systems, and compliance across Latin America and the Caribbean. Culminated in a 260-page IDB report published in April 2026.

Completed · 15 countries · 2025–2026

Water Quality Compliance in Brazil

Econometric analysis of compliance with drinking water quality standards in Brazil across three dimensions: reporting compliance, sampling adequacy, and violation rates.

In progress · Brazil · Working paper

Water Access Inequality Under Drought: Mexico City

Examines distributional impacts of the 2024 water emergency in Mexico City, documenting how drought-driven scarcity amplifies existing socioeconomic inequalities in access to safe drinking water.

In progress · Mexico · Urban

Water Infrastructure Resilience to Natural Disasters

Assesses the vulnerability and resilience of water and sanitation infrastructure to floods and landslides in Colombia, using pipe break data.

In progress · Colombia · 2025–2027
